Complete Chess Rules

The Setup

Chess is played on an 8x8 board. White always moves first. Players alternate single turns.


Piece Movement

  • King (♔): Moves exactly 1 square in any direction.
  • Queen (♕): Moves any number of vacant squares in any direction (diagonally, horizontally, or vertically).
  • Rook (♖): Moves any number of vacant squares horizontally or vertically.
  • Bishop (♗): Moves any number of vacant squares diagonally.
  • Knight (♘): Moves in an "L" shape (two squares in one direction, then one square at a 90-degree angle). Can jump over other pieces.
  • Pawn (♙): Moves forward exactly 1 square. On its first move, it can move forward 2 squares. Captures exactly 1 square diagonally forward.

Special Rules

  • Castling: A simultaneous move of the King and Rook. The King moves 2 squares toward the Rook, and the Rook jumps over the King. Allowed only if neither piece has moved, the path is clear, and the King is not in, moving through, or landing in Check.
  • En Passant: If a pawn moves 2 squares on its first move and lands beside an enemy pawn, that enemy pawn can capture it on the very next turn as if it had only moved 1 square.
  • Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it must be instantly swapped for a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight of the same color.

Winning & Drawing

  • Check: The King is under immediate attack. You MUST move the King, block the attack, or capture the attacker.
  • Checkmate: The King is in Check and has no legal escape. The game is over.
  • Stalemate (Draw): The player whose turn it is is NOT in Check, but has absolutely no legal moves left. The game is a tie.
